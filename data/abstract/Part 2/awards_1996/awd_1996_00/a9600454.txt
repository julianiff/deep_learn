Abstract    :
              Dr. Giovanetti and his undergraduate students from James  Madison University
              (JMU) are participating in the  construction, and ultimately the usage, of the
              CEBAF Large  Acceptance Spectrometer (CLAS) at the newly operating  Continuous
              Electron Beam Accelerator Facility (CEBAF) in  Newport News, VA. Presently the
              group is constructing and  testing a system to monitor the gains of a major
              detector  system for CLAS (>1000 photomultiplier tubes). The CLAS  detector
              should begin to operate next year, and the JMU  group will participate in
              experiments to study the quark  gluon structure of baryons. This program
              provides excellent  opportunities for undergraduate students to participate in 
              forefront research. The close proximity of JMU to CEBAF also  provides the
              students rather extensive involvement in the  research by permitting a number
              of visits to the lab in a  cost effective manner.
