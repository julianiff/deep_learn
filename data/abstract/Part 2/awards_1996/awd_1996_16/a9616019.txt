Abstract    :
              In this project in the Physical Chemistry Program of the Chemistry   Division,  
              Scott Auerbach of the Department of Chemistry of   the   University of 
              Massachusetts-Amherst  will  model the microscopic   dynamical processes that
              control hydrocarbon mobility and reactivity in   X-  and Y-zeolites. 
              Specifically benzene hopping, diffusion and   reorientation rates will be
              calculated for different substituted   zeolites.  The proton transfer rates
              between the oxygens and adsorbed   butene in acidic zeolites will be simulated.
              The proton dynamics   calculations will help understanding the mechanism of 
              formation of   hydrocarbons, which may suggest ways to improve the performance
              of these   materials.  The aim is to develop better understanding for the
              fundamental processes   involved in the production of hydrocarbons in zeolites.
              This is of   importance in the production of hydrocarbons from non-traditional 
               sources.
