Abstract    :
                   The experimental physics group at John's Hopkins  proposes to continue
              their experimental program at the CDF  detector at Fermilab.  This collider
              experiment has been  highly successful, recently discovering the last of the 
              currently expected quarks, the top-quark.  This experiment  is at the energy
              frontier in particle physics, and is now  being upgraded to operate at higher
              beam intensities.  Hopkins is playing an important role in the experiment and 
              the upgrade.  This upgrade will allow more stringent tests  of the standard
              model and gives the experiment enhanced  discovery potential.       The Hopkins
              group has begun to orient themselves  towards the proposed CMS experiment at
              CERN.  Their  experience at Fermilab should make them a valuable  contributor
              at the energy frontier of the future.
