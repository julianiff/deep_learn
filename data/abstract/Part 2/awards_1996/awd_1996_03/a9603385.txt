Abstract    :
              The goal of this collaboration between the PI and David Blaschke at the
              University of   Rostock involving senior investigators, postdoctoral fellows,
              and students is to study and   derive effective models for non-perturbative
              quantum chromodynamics at finite   temperature and baryon density.  This
              proposal unites the U.S. group with its expertise in   Dyson-Schwinger
              equations and model field theories with the German group, who are   experts in
              many-body techniques at finite temperature.  The collaboration produces a  
              group that is ideally suited to discover those observables that may provide
              characteristic   signatures of quark-gluon plasma formation.  The collaborative
              exchange visits are   designed to facilitate rapid progress through intensive
              periods of close interaction.  The   education and training of students and
              postdoctoral fellows is a significant aspect of this   proposal.
