Abstract    :
              Sonoluminescence occurs when a bubble of air in a fluid bath, periodically
              driven by a   sound wave, emits light.  The major theoretical challenge in
              understanding   sonoluminescence experiments is to determine how much of the
              phenomenon is   hydrodynamic, how much is atomic, and how much is chemical. 
              This proposal is for joint   collaborative work between the PI's group and
              physicists from the University of Marburg   to uncover the hydrodynamic
              parameters underlying sonluminescence, both with respect   of bubble dynamics
              and the hydrodynamic mechanism for the energy focusing.
