Abstract    :
              This study of the Human Genome Project will use the tools and perspectives of
              history,  sociology, and anthropology to analyze rapid scientific and
              technological developments  in contemporary human genetics, the organizational
              changes necessary to keep pace with  those developments, the recent growth of
              commercial ventures in genomics, and the challenges  to ethical and social
              analysis presented by such a fast-changing and complex set of processes.  
              Using archival materials, fieldwork, and in-depth interviews with scientists in
              industry and  academe, the project will examine the impact of recombinant DNA
              methods in the field of  human genetics, and the resulting demands for a
              coordinated effort to map and sequence the  entire human genome.  The final
              product will be a book, titled Inventing Speed Genomics, that  will analyze
              such developments and contribute to public discussion about genetics research
              and  its social meanings.
