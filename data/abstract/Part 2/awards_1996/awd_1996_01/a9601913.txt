Abstract    :
              Science has had a powerful transformative influence on contemporary U.S.
              cultural, challenging  and reshaping traditional social values.  In turn, U.S.
              social and political values have shaped the  course of science in ways that
              have not yet been fully examined.  Focusing on the cultural  authority of
              science, this research will combine two traditions of historical research to
              examine  the reciprocal influences of science and culture in the contemporary
              U.S.  One tradition is  organizational in character, concentrating on the
              connections between expert knowledge and the  state.  The second tradition
              examines the ways that scientific knowledge itself responds to the  cultural
              context in which it is undertaken.  The results of this work will be published
              in a book,  currently under contract with the Cambridge University Press.
