Abstract    :
              This starter grant award to Stanford University supports the research of  
              Professor Thomas J. Wandless.  The research focuses on two elements of   the
              cytoskeleton of cells: gamma-tubulin, a protein implicated in   microtubule
              nucleation, and FtsZ, a bacterial protein essential for   bacterial cell
              division.  The research undertakes the expression and   purification of
              gamma-tubulin, the investigation of gamma-tubulin as a   biologically-relevant
              receptor for the antimitotic anticancer drugs, the   determination of whether
              gamma-tubulin is able to nucleate microtuble   formation in vitro, and the
              analysis of FtsZ as a possible receptor for   antimitotic drugs as well as a
              structural model for the tubulin family.  The research will advance the
              fundamental understanding of eukaryotic   cytoskeletal structure and function
              and may point to new strategies for   the treatment of human disease.
