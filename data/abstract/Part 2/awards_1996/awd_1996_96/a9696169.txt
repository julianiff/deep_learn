Abstract    :
                This three-year award will support U.S.-France cooperative research in
              biological oceanography between Lisa Campbell of the University of Hawaii and
              Daniel Vaulot  of the University of Paris VI.  The objective of their research
              is to develop useful probes for identification and enumeration of important
              picoeukaryotic algae. Picoeukaryotic algae are the very smallest eukaryotic
              phytoplankton and contribute a large percentage of primary production in
              tropical and subtropic oceans.  The investigators will develop and apply
              oligonucleotide probes and immunochemical probes to cell surface antigens.   
              Mutual benefits of this project include obtaining the best available probes to
              date and devising the best strategy to address the overall objectives. It is
              expected that results from this project will advance our knowledge of the
              composition and community structure of picoeukaryotic algae.
