Abstract    :
              The northern Appalachians contain a number of exotic terranes, accreted during 
              the closure of the Iapetus ocean that involved elements of North America and
              Laurentia as well as  Gondwana.  However, the timing, geometry and terrane
              positions are still incompletely known.  This  project will focus on the
              western margin of Avalon, one of the more important terranes, in Maine, and 
              the Maritime provinces of eastern Canada and will involve paleomagnetic,
              geochronologic and tectonic  investigations.  Results will be useful in testing
              the large number of contrasting tectonic scenarios  that have been proposed for
              the Appalachian Caledonian belt, in particular the tectonic setting and 
              locations of major arc/back arc systems in the southern portion of Iapetus.
