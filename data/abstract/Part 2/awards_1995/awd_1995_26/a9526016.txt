Abstract    :
              Recent geologic and geodetic data indicate that a zone, termed the Eastern
              California  shear zone, of right lateral shear has been long-lived and is still
              active.  However, within the shear  zone, there is conflicting evidence of
              which strand is active-geologic data require that the Death  Valley-Furnace
              Creek fault zone is the principal locus of shear, while geodetic data appear to
              favor  the Owens Valley fault.  Of the several possible explanations of this
              paradox,  this project will test  the model that the locus of shear is
              currently in the process of shifting from the former to the  later.  The
              results are also expected to improve understanding of the fundamental
              mechanisms that  control fault interactions and slip partitioning between
              parallel strike-slip faults and connecting  normal faults.
