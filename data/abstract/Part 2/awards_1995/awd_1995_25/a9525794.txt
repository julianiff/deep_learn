Abstract    :
              This project explores the politics of collective memory or commemoration in
              science by  comparing the earliest anniversary celebrations of American,
              British and French molecular  biologists.  Commemorative events are viewed as
              rites of succession, in which the passing  generation gives place to the
              upcoming elite.  Also of concern is the way that scientists'  individual,
              private and even inconsequential memories are transformed into collective,
              public  and politically-useful commemorations.  Considerable attention will be
              given to the  characteristics of those persons and events that have been
              excluded from commemoration.  To  accomplish these ends I will examine
              published and unpublished documents, including  correspondence of key figures,
              and will conduct oral histories and ethnographic field studies of  recent
              commemorative events.
