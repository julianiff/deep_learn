Abstract    :
               This project concentrates on three areas:           (1)  Average-case
              complexity: Tuning up the average-case  reduction theory, finding  more
              average-case complete  problems, and proving average-case hardness of real- 
              life problems;           (2) Finite model theory: Investigating whether 
              semantical  properties (such as monotonicity) have any syntactic 
              characterizations on finite structures, determining  whether parts of
              polynomial  time properties can be  captured by polynomial-time-bounded logics,
              and  studying finite structures with weights in infinite  structures (such as 
              arithmetic);           (3) Evolving algebras: Examining a new computation model
               (evolving algebras) in order to use these algebras to  specify and verify 
              real-life algorithms.
