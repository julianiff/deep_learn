Abstract    :
              This standard grant will provide partial support for two  graduate students to
              supervise the construction and  commissioning of the ICEBall-2 mini-orange
              spectrometers  within the Gammasphere array, a premier detection system  used
              by a large consortium of nuclear-structure physicists.  This large gamma-ray
              detector array is used to study the  complex level structure of nuclei in order
              to understand the  underlying nuclear structure.  The mini-orange spectrometers
               allow measurements of internal conversion electrons, which  result from
              electromagnetic transitions between levels for  which there is no change in
              angular momentum, which  typically arise from interband transitions.  Observing
              these  transitions thus allows a more complete understanding of the  level
              schemes.  The research using this device will be  performed at the ATLAS
              facility at Argonne National  Laboratory.
