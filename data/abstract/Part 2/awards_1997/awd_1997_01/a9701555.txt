Abstract    :
              Suchan, Trudy  Pennsylvania State University   Categories are necessary for
              making sense of the everyday world of the individual for purposes of spatial
              analysis and mapping.  This doctoral dissertation research will focus on the
              internal nature of a category for which data are often aggregated, analyzed and
              mapped-rural.  The case study explores, through data collected by in-depth
              interviews, common mental categorizations of rural, and compares these to the
              formalized conceptions that underlie maps about rural issues.  Informal and
              formal definitions are examined in terms of prototype category theory. 
              Implications of identified differences are discussed, particularly when the
              formal categories are the basis of information presented to the public.
