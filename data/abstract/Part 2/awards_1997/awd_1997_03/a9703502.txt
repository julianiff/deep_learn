Abstract    :
              Ivester, Andrew  University of Georgia   The doctoral dissertation research will
              determine the ages of dune activity in Georgia, describe the geomorphic and
              stratigraphic context of the dunes, and use the resulting information to infer
              the processes and environmental conditions that produced the dunes.  Ages of
              dune activity will be determined using optically stimulated luminescence (OSL)
              dating with supplementary age data from accelerator mass spectrometer (AMS)
              radiocarbon dating.  Results will yield a record of natural climatic
              variability and illustrate the effects of global climate fluctuations on eolian
              activity.
