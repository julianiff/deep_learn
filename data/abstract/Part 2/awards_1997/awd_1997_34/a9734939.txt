Abstract    :
              The  energetics of dislocations moving with acceleration are  studied  which 
              are  important  for  the  understanding   of  micromechanisms  underlying  the 
              dynamic   deformation   of  ductile  materials  under high strain  rates.  The 
              Peierls-  Nabarro   model  has  earlier  been  succesful  for   static 
              deformations  and  is  being extended to  dynamic  transient  motion. Issues of
              generation and motion of dislocations near  moving   shock   fronts  and  the 
              motion   of   twins   are  addressed.
