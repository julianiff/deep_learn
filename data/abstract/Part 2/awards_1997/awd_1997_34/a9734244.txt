Abstract    :
              The objective of this integrated education and research plan is to augment
              damping in large civil structures applications, via both passive and active
              means, to reduce structural response.  The proposed research program will
              develop, analyze and experimentally demonstrate passive, semi-active, and
              active structural damping control for civil structures using smart materials
              and structures technology.  The research builds on the ongoing research efforts
              at Maryland on stability augmentation, shock, and vibration control using
              electrorheological (ER) and magnetorheological (MR) dampers.  Damping
              strategies will be tested on dynamically scaled three story civil structures
              building model currently under development.
