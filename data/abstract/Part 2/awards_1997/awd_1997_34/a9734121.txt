Abstract    :
              The modeling and numerical approximation of the anomalous  diffusion of low
              molecular weight penetrates in glassy  polymeric systems is developed. This is
              a coupled physics  problem that involves the theory of deformable mechanics, 
              nonlinear diffusion, and polymer chemistry Case II diffusion  is analyzed.
              Areas of application include lithography and  adhesive failure. Innovation in
              teaching in mechanics and  materials is provided through the use of
              interdisciplinary  problems addressed in the research. Graduate education is 
              enhanced through mentoring and modernization of course work  to reflect
              practical methodologies for solution of complex  mechanics problems.
