Abstract    :
                This project will culture planktic foraminifera to address questions
              concerning the controls upon incorporation of stable isotopes and trace
              elements in the calcite skelteon.  Topics to be addressed include: the role of
              seawater carbonate chemistry and of temperature in isotopic ratios in warm-and
              cold-water taxa, the relationship of Mg concentration to temperature, and the
              relation of Cd concentration to nutrient content. When the environmental
              controls on these tracers are better understood, it will be possible to use the
              geochemistry of fossil foraminifera to produce more accurate quantitative
              estimates of ocean and climate changes.  The estimates can be used to test the
              accuracy of climate models in simulating climate under various conditions such
              as those of the recent past.
