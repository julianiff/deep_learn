Abstract    :
              The focus of this research is the preparation and characterization of  
              carbon-rich networks based on polyacetylenic benzenoid aromatics.  Both  
              oligomeric substructures and polymers will be constructed  and   characterized.
               Target systems include graphdiyne, graphyne and   triacetylenic  macrocycles. 
              With this award, the Synthetic Organic   Program is supporting the research of 
              Dr. Michael M. Haley of the   Department of Chemistry at the University of
              Oregon.  Dr. Haley will   focus his work on the preparation of densely
              cross-linked carbon-rich   solids based on polyacetylenic benzenoid
              polyaromatics.  These polymeric   networks have the potential for having
              properties amenable to use as   advanced structural materials for nuclear power
              generators, gas turbine   engines, high performance heat engines, heat
              exchangers, cutting tools,   glassy carbon electrodes, and aerospace
              applications.
