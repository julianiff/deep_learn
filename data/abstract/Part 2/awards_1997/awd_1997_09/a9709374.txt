Abstract    :
              Evolutionary models of dynamics have been a source of robust explanations of
              individual behavior in experiments and in the development of social norms. 
              This project synthesizes these two applications as experiments are conducted on
              populations of individuals who choose between idiosyncratic and aggregate
              gambles.  This research is based upon the work of Arthur Robson which shows
              that there are systematic biases in the evolutionary dynamics toward the
              selection of idiosyncratic gambles.  If these models prove to be empirically
              robust, then we can confidently apply these evolutionary models to topics such
              as understanding how markets for risky assets have developed.
