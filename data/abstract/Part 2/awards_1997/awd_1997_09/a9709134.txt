Abstract    :
              In this proposal, the P. I.  will investigate a series of
              measurement-theoretical issues clustered around three types of decision-making
              problems:   optimization problems,   consensus problems, and  judgments of
              closeness.    For each class of problems, the P.I. lists a series of important
              applications, and identifies a series of unsolved theoretical questions, most
              of them related to the issue of meaningfulness.  These research questions
              evolve around the question of whether the solution to an optimization or
              consensus problem or a particular judgment of closeness or distance is
              `meaningful,` in the sense that it is not an artifact of the particular
              measurement scale on which input variables were assessed.
