Abstract    :
              A new, finite-element-like computational methodology called 
              Variable-Element-Topology Finite Element Method (VETFEM) is  being developed
              that accounts for applications in solid  mechanics that involve a change in the
              topology of the  material domain. Such problems include: fracture, 
              penetration, material removal such as machining, shape  optimization, metal
              forming, etc. The VETFEM does not  require topologically similar elements and
              thus allows  suitable meshes to be formed from a predefined distribution  of
              node points. The VETFEM is being developed for large  dynamic deformations and
              inelastic response. The validation  is done using problems drawn from areas of
              material failure  and manufacturing processes.
