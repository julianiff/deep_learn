Abstract    :
                A careful analysis of upper ocean field data collected during two cruises
              (1995   and 1997) funded under the Coastal Ocean Process (CoOP) program will be
                conducted to estimate air-sea gas exchanges. A focus of the investigation
              will be   on the interactive role that atmospheric forcing and surfactants play
              generating   and damping small waves and near-surface turbulence, and how the  
              modification of these processes affects the transfer velocity and therefore the
                turbulent gas flux. The expected outcome of the analyses is an improved  
              parameterization of the air-sea gas transfer velocity which incorporates all
              the   relevant physical and chemical processes, and is applicable to wide range
              of   environmental conditions.
