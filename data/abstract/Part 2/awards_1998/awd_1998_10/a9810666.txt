Abstract    :
                This project will document the Hupa, Yurok, and Karok languages of Northwest
              California, producing a cross linguistic dictionary containing (1) grammatical
              analysis of word forms and (2) examples of words in sentences. This dictionary
              will be used to investigate cross linguistic variation in the conceptual
              content of words, grammatical categories, and sentences.  Methodology will
              consist of (1) documenting vocabulary, grammatical units, and narrative by
              working with native speakers; and (2) consolidating existing materials in each
              of the languages within a unified computerized database.   The project will
              contribute to (1 ) the descriptive knowledge of the Hupa, Yurok. and Karok
              lanugages, all of which are endangered; and (2) the theoretical understanding
              of the role grammatical categories play in the conceptual organization of human
              language, including linguistic concepts of space, time, metaphor, taxonomic
              classes, and other distinctions between basic concepts of'object'/'event' and
              'subject'/'object'.
