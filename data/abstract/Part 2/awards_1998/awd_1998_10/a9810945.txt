Abstract    :
              This dissertation research examines the role that migration plays in the
              adjustment of local labor markets when those labor markets experience
              structural changes. The research is organized around one central hypothesis,
              which states that the importance of migration as an adjustment mechanism varies
              widely across industrial sectors and occupations. Thus, the total amount of
              migration into or out of a local area depends not so much on the total change
              in employment as on the distribution of that change over different industrial
              or occupational categories.  The study is therefore concerned with the balance
              between migration and local adjustments, and the results will provide insight
              into structural changes in local space economies.
