Abstract    :
              The focus of this research is the development of C-C- bond making   methodology
              using eta-3-allylmolybdenum complexes containing a chelating   nucleophile and
              a heteroatom bound to the metal center.   The complexes   react with
              electrophiles, such as aldehydes, with a high degree of both   regio and
              diastereoselectivity.   With this renewal award, the Organic   and
              Macromolecular Chemistry Program is supporting the research of  Dr.   Marie E.
              Krafft of the Department of Chemistry at Florida State   University.  Professor
              Krafft will focus her work on the development of   C-C bond forming methodology
              using the reactions of molybdenum complexes   with electrophiles.  The
              methodology has potential impact for the   synthesis of natural products. 
              Further, the research provides a valuable   training ground for both graduate
              and undergraduate students.
