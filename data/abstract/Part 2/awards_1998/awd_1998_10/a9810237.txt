Abstract    :
                This doctoral dissertation research examines how non-political grass-roots
              organizations affect patterns of political participation in poor communities in
              Mexico.  The researcher hypothesizes that the internal characteristics of the
              associations, as well as the local context within which they operate, has a
              direct linkage to patterns of political participation.  The researcher has
              selected a matched sample of communities in Mexico and has coded different
              local associations for the degree to which they encourage open decision making.
               The openness and closedness of decision making is correlated with voting
              turnout, political party involvement and archival information about levels of
              participation.  This study posits a causal linkage between non-political
              associational activity and political involvement that is important for
              understanding democratic political change in rural settings.
