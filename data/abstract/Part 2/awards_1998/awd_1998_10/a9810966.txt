Abstract    :
              Charles Darwin is best known as a biologist, yet in his early career his
              referred to himself as a geologist.  The purposes of this project are to
              understand the meaning and significance of this self-identification.  To do so,
              I propose to study Darwin's geological manuscripts in the Cambridge University
              Library and  his geological specimens held in the Sedgwick Museum in Cambridge,
              England, and to visit the field sites important to the development of glacial
              theory that involved Darwin.  This research will yield a book that will examine
              Darwin's geology and trace its connections with his thinking about species.
