Abstract    :
                   The subject of atom interferometry, the ability to  split and then
              recombine matter waves, has great promise for  measuring weak interactions and
              for ultra high precision  measurements.  Several topics related to this subject
              will  be explored.  They include methods for focussing an atomic  beam to a
              single spot, Bragg scattering of atoms by  counterpropagating optical fields
              and the use of Rydberg  atoms to construct a new type of atom interferometer.
