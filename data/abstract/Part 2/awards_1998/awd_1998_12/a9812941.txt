Abstract    :
              A study will be made of the fundamental nature of boundary lubrication of
              biological contacts.  Friction force microscopy will be used to study the
              boundary layer lubricant properties of biological fluids at the molecular
              level.  It is hypothesized that chain molecules of poly (ethylene oxide), poly
              (ethylene glycol), and hyaluronic acid will passivate the solid surface and
              play the roles of additives in industrial lubricants.  Surfaces passivated by
              these layers will be opposed to a family of reference biomaterial surfaces at
              well-defined, molecular-scale separations and the friction forces will be
              determined.  Atomically smooth surfaces will also be studied.  Variables will
              be actual surface area, normal load, adhesion, and frequency of deformation.
