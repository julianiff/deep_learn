Abstract    :
              The accurate prediction of damage and delamination in  composite automotive,
              aerospace, and marine structures under  impact loading is addressed. The
              mechanisms that play an  important role in the development of damage zone that 
              eventually leads to interfacial fracture, and how the shape  and size of the
              damage and delamination zone relates to the  loading conditions and material
              constituents are studied. A  micromechanics based damage model, a set of
              kinetic  equations for the evolution of fracture and delamination,  and a
              finite deformation composite shell theory are  developed.
