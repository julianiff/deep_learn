Abstract    :
                This research proposes to examine how legislative leaders help their parties
              shape the issue agenda outside of the legislature.  While the process by which
              issues are framed internal to legislatures is reasonably well understood, what
              is less clear is how issues are presented external to a legislature and how the
              media's attention is focused on those issues.  This research focuses on the
              strategic considerations of party leaders, party members and the media in
              presenting and resisting issues in an information rich setting.  New techniques
              for content analysis are brought to bear on a large amount of data collected by
              the PI.
