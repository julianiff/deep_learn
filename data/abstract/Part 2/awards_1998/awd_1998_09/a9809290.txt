Abstract    :
                This doctoral dissertation research examines the root causes of inter-racial
              attitudinal differences.  A large body of research in political science and in
              social psychology demonstrates that there are important differences in
              attitudes both within and across different racial and ethnic groups.  These
              differences have important implications for the support of governing
              institutions.  This research relies on laboratory experiments to discern
              whether subjects who have high attachment to social groups process information
              through a racial schema.  The experiments require subjects to recall news
              stories in either printed or videotaped form that have been altered to produce
              different types of messages.  The research isolates one mechanism which, when
              coupled with racial group identification, aids in the formulation of individual
              perceptions and attitudes.
