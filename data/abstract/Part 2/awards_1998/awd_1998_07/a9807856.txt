Abstract    :
              The as milled states of Fe-Zn materials obtained by ball  milling of elemental
              powders of appropriate amounts are  characterized. The study contributes to the
              understanding of  far from equilibrium transformation kinetics associated with 
              intermetallics, and their relation to the re-assessment of  equilibrium phase
              diagram. The origin, and nature of the  induced magnetism associated with ball
              milling of Zn-rich  FeZn base materials is evaluated. The results of this 
              studyare valuable in fabricating cheap magnets for  appliances, and in better
              control of coated steels for  automotive panels, nuts, bolts, beams, etc.
