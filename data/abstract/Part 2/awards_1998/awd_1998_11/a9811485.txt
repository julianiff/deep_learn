Abstract    :
              Support is provided for a two-day symposium on social identity, intergroup
              conflict, and conflict reduction. This is the third program in the biennial
              Rutgers Series on Self and Social Identity. The purpose of the symposium is to
              elucidate the many ways in which identity and intergroup conflict are
              interrelated. The goal is to increase understanding both of social identity and
              intergroup harm doing. The symposium will also address ways to reduce
              intergroup conflict by taking into account the role of social identity. The
              symposium will take place in April, 1999 on the Douglass Campus of Rutgers
              University. An edited volume based on the symposium will be published by Oxford
              University Press.
