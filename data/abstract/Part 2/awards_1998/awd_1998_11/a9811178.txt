Abstract    :
              This dissertation research will evaluate human/environmental relationships on
              the coastal plain of the Atlantic coast of the United States during the
              Holocene.  Radiocarbon and thermoluminescence dating techniques will be used on
              peat deposits and underlying sands sampled in the regions of interest.  In
              conjunction with pollen analysis and the examination of archaeological data
              from neighboring areas, the investigators hope to produce accurate dates for
              vegetation change and human settlements in three research sites. The study will
              contribute to our understanding of the environmental, ecological, and
              agricultural history of the Coastal Plain, and in so doing, it promises to
              provide evidence of human responses to and modification of the `natural`
              environment during the Archaic period.
