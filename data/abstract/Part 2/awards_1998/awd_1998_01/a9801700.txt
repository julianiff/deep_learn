Abstract    :
              Anne-Marie Broudehoux  University of California, Berkeley   The doctoral
              dissertation research proposal investigates the process of image making in
              Beijing, the Chinese capital city, from the beginning of the reform era to the
              current day.  The overall objective is to seek to understand the spatial
              construction of identity in the context of China's transition from planned to
              market economy and the impact of urban redevelopment of the city on the
              reconfiguration of Chinese society and its experience of modernity.  Three case
              studies of urban space will be used to assess the image formation process.  The
              project relies on written and pictorial evidence as well as on ethnographic
              investigation in the extant urban fabric of Beijing.
