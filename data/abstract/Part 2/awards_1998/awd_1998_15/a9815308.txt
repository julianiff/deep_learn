Abstract    :
              With National Science Foundation support, Mr. Chris Koonooka will attend the
              University of Washington's archaeological field school to be held on Kodiak
              Island (Alaska) from June 20 to August 10, 1998 The program will be conducted
              at and around the Tanginak Spring site and is designed to give students a broad
              range of training in archaeological techniques. Field instruction will include
              training in archaeological surveying, mapping, excavation, note-taking and
              photography. Laboratory instruction will focus on cleaning, cataloging,
              artifact analysis and elementary computer mapping. Mr. Koonooka is a Native
              Alaskan from St. Lawrence Island and is enrolled in the Department of Education
              at the University of Alaska, Fairbanks. He will be the first member of the St.
              Lawrence community to receive formal training in archaeology and will return to
              the island to teach young people what he has learned.   Although Native
              American prehistory has formed an important archaeological focus for well over
              a century, almost no Native Americans have participated in the research
              process. This grant is part of a larger initiative by the NSF Archaeology
              Program to change this situation. St. Lawrence Island contains important
              archaeological resources and it will be extremely important to have an
              individual such as Mr. Koonooka involved in their management.
