Abstract    :
              University of Wisconsin, Madison   The proposed research represents a
              theoretical and philosophical investigation of the concept of geographical
              judgement, divided into instrumental judgement (the usefulness of places for
              completing social projects; projects of geographical practice) and intrinsic
              judgement (the clarity of insight into the world that is afforded by geography
              as a science). The tensions between these are investigated with case studies of
              selected places, and conclusions about moral reasoning in geography will be
              drawn. Building on his previous work, the PI wishes to develop and
              operationalize a reconceptualized notion of `place` in relation to processes of
              judgement.
